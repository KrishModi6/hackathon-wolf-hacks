TECH ARCHITECTURE

How the engine actually works.

Input layer: Voice (Web Speech) / Text / SMS gateway -> normalized symptom set.
Classifier: CTAS rule baseline -> Safety floor (never below CTAS 2 if anchor symptoms present) -> ML patterns (P(cardiac), P(stroke), P(sepsis), P(anaphylaxis)).
Demographic mods: Pediatric, geriatric, pregnancy, chronic conditions - matches CTAS Pediatric / Obstetric modifiers.
Routing: Capability hard-filter -> M/M/c wait predictor -> weighted score (0.5 sev + 0.3 wait + 0.2 dist) + scenario boosts.
Handoff: Token-signed QR; offline-ready card for paramedic/nurse intake.

Q&A Preparation - Hard Questions

Q: What if your AI misclassifies a heart attack as indigestion?

A: It can't. We have a one-way safety floor: any anchor symptom (chest pain, breathing trouble, stroke signs, severe bleeding, unconsciousness) forces CTAS 2 or below regardless of any other input. The ML pattern layer can only ESCALATE further, never relax. We also require a CTAS-certified physician to review the rules before pilot - that's part of our Ask.

Q: Patient privacy under PHIPA?

A: PHIPA-compliant by design. The QR token is short-lived (in-memory, restart-bounded), no PHI is logged in our analytics (only CTAS counts and timestamps), and any production deployment would route through Ontario Health's certified data residency. See the Constraints page for the full compliance map.

Q: What about patients without smartphones?

A: Our SMS fallback (see /sms-demo) works on any phone with text. Same triage engine, same routing, same QR card via MMS. We also designed the form to be screen-reader-friendly and use a Lexend font that's tested for dyslexia accessibility.

Q: How do you handle multilingual residents (Punjabi, Urdu, Tamil)?

A: The AI assistant accepts free-text in any language OpenAI supports - we've tested Punjabi and Urdu. The structured form is currently English-only but we partnered with Punjabi Community Health Services in our stakeholder list for a translated v2.

Q: What's stopping a hospital from just rejecting the QR card?

A: Nothing today - it's an advisory tool, not a binding intake. That's why our Phase 1 pilot is a single walk-in clinic with explicit MOU. The card never bypasses clinical judgment; it just speeds up intake.

Q: What happens during a real Condition X outbreak in 2026?

A: The AI assistant detects outbreak signals automatically (e.g. "many people sick", "everyone in my school is out") and flips Condition X mode. Non-critical patients are auto-routed to virtual triage, ER waits triple in the model so users see realistic expectations, and the dashboard shows redirected-patient counts in real time.
